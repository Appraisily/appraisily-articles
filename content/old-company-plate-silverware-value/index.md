## 1. Understanding Old Company Plate Silverware Value  
Old Company Plate refers to electroplated nickel silver (EPNS) flatware produced primarily in the mid-20th century. Unlike sterling silver (92.5% pure silver), these pieces feature a thin silver layer over base metals. Value derives from:  

- **Collector demand** for specific patterns like Signature Rose (Poshmark listings show $15-$75 for individual pieces[2][17])  
- **Historical manufacturing significance** as part of America's silverplate industry boom[3][14]  
- **Design appeal** of Art Deco and Mid-Century Modern styles  
- **Utilitarian value** for vintage-inspired table settings  

While melt value is negligible, complete sets in good condition can reach $200-$400 at auction[2][6]. Monogrammed pieces often carry 10-20% premium[2][13].  

## 2. Historical Context & Development  
The brand emerged during America's silverplate golden age (1920s-1960s), when companies like:
