## 1. Defining Murano Glass and the Threat of Counterfeits  

### 1.1 What Constitutes Authentic Murano Glass  
Authentic Murano glass refers to glassworks created on the Venetian island of Murano using traditional techniques perfected since the 13th century. Key hallmarks include:  
- **Handmade craftsmanship**: Each piece exhibits minor imperfections like air bubbles formed during layered glass blowing[1][5].  
- **Pontil marks**: A small scar on the base from the glassblower's rod, detectable through tactile inspection[1][5].  
- **Non-toxic materials**: Absence of lead, with even clear glass showing slight opacity due to soda-lime composition[1][5].  
- **Vibrant coloration**: Mastery of techniques like *sommerso* (layered colors) and *millefiori* (floral patterns)[4][9].
