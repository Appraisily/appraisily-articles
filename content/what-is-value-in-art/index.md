## 1. Defining Value in Art  
**Value** operates on two primary levels:  
- **Technical**: The lightness/darkness scale in visual composition, crucial for creating depth and contrast[1][8]. For example, Rembrandt’s chiaroscuro technique uses dramatic value shifts to highlight emotional intensity.  
- **Cultural/Market**: The monetary, historical, or symbolic worth assigned to artworks. Leonardo da Vinci’s *Salvator Mundi* sold for $450.3 million, reflecting its rarity and historical significance[2][12].  

This duality makes value essential for both artistic expression (e.g., guiding viewer focus) and socioeconomic ecosystems (e.g., investment markets)[8][13].
