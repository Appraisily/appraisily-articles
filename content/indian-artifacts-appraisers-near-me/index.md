Indian artifact appraisers specialize in evaluating the authenticity, provenance, and market value of Native American cultural objects, including tools, pottery, textiles, and ceremonial items. - **Legal Compliance**: Adhering to laws like the Native American Graves Protection and Repatriation Act (NAGPRA), which governs the handling of sacred objects[6][14].  
- **Insurance and Estate Planning**: Establishing fair market values for insurance claims or inheritance purposes[2][9].  
- **Cultural Stewardship**: Preventing the circulation of looted or forged items by verifying authenticity through material analysis and provenance research[6][12].

## 2. Historical Development of Artifact Appraisal Practices  

The formalization of artifact appraisal emerged alongside the 20th-century antiquities market, as private collectors and institutions sought expertise to navigate growing concerns over authenticity.
