# The Comprehensive Guide to the Value of West Point "W" Mint Mark Quarters  

The West Point "W" mint mark quarters, first released in 2019 and 2020, represent a significant modern numismatic innovation. These quarters, part of the U.S. Mint’s America the Beautiful series, were intentionally produced in limited quantities to reignite public interest in coin collecting. With only 2 million examples struck per design and distributed randomly into circulation, their scarcity and historical significance have made them highly sought after. Values range from $15 for lower-grade examples to over $40,000 for pristine specimens, driven by condition, design rarity, and collector demand. This report explores the origins, varieties, valuation factors, and market dynamics of these distinctive coins.

## Historical Background and Development  

### Origins of the West Point Mint  
The West Point Mint, established in 1937, initially served as a bullion depository rather than a coin production facility[1][10]. Its role expanded in 1974 when it began striking Lincoln cents, though these lacked mint marks and were indistinguishable from Philadelphia issues[1]. The first coins bearing the "W" mint mark emerged in 1983 with commemorative gold $10 coins for the Los Angeles Olympics[1]. However, it wasn’t until 2019 that the West Point Mint produced its first circulating quarters, marking a deliberate shift to engage collectors through scarcity[8][10].  

### The 2019–2020 America the Beautiful Initiative  
In April 2019, the U.S. Mint announced the release of 10 million "W" quarters (2 million per design) into general circulation, a historic move coinciding with National Coin Week[8][10]. This initiative aimed to democratize access to rare coins, allowing everyday individuals to discover them in pocket change. The 2019 designs honored Lowell National Historical Park (Massachusetts), American Memorial Park (Northern Mariana Islands), War in the Pacific National Historical Park (Guam), San Antonio Missions National Historical Park (Texas), and Frank Church River of No Return Wilderness (Idaho)[2][8]. In 2020, five additional designs were released, including Weir Farm National Historic Site (Connecticut) and Salt River Bay (U.S. Virgin Islands), each also limited to 2 million pieces[3][5].

## Types and Variations  

### Design-Specific Quarters  
The 2019 and 2020 "W" quarters feature unique reverse designs tied to national parks and historic sites:  
- **2019 Series**: Highlights include the San Antonio Missions’ Greek cross motif and the Frank Church Wilderness’s driftboat scene[5][8].  
- **2020 Series**: Notable designs include the Marsh-Billings-Rockefeller National Historical Park’s seedling planting and the Tallgrass Prairie’s butterfly motif[3][5].  

### The 2020 V75 Privy Mark  
Quarters minted in 2020 bear a small "V75" privy mark on the obverse, commemorating the 75th anniversary of Allied victory in World War II[3][14]. This mark, located near Washington’s bust, adds historical layering and has been shown to increase value by up to 30% compared to non-privy counterparts[3][14].

## Factors Determining Value  

### Condition and Grading  
Coins are graded on the Sheldon Scale (1–70), with "mint state" (MS60+) examples commanding premiums. For instance:  
- A 2019 W Lowell quarter graded MS60 is valued at $15, while an MS67 example reaches $375[1].  
- The 2020 W Weir Farm quarter jumps from $35 in MS65 to $1,400 in MS67[1].  
Exceptional specimens, such as a 2020 W Salt River Bay quarter graded MS68, have sold for nearly $6,000[1].  

### Rarity and Mintage  
With only 2 million struck per design, "W" quarters are far scarcer than Philadelphia (P) or Denver (D) mint issues, which often exceed 100 million[1][8]. For example, the 2019 W San Antonio Missions quarter has a mintage 57 times smaller than its Denver counterpart[6].  

### Design Popularity  
Certain reverse designs attract higher demand due to aesthetic or historical appeal. The 2020 W American Samoa quarter, depicting a flying fox, has consistently outperformed others in auctions, with MS67 examples selling for $650[1][3].  

### Errors and Varieties  
Minting errors, such as misaligned strikes or doubled dies, can exponentially increase value. A 2019 W American Memorial Park quarter with a doubled obverse sold for $2,000 in 2021[6].

## Identification and Authentication  

### Locating the "W" Mint Mark  
The mint mark appears on the obverse below "IN GOD WE TRUST" and to the right of Washington’s neck[3][5]. Circulated examples may show wear on high points (e.g., Washington’s hair), reducing grade and value[1][14].  

### Assessing Authenticity  
- **Weight and Composition**: Authentic quarters weigh 5.67 grams and have a diameter of 24.26 mm[7][9].  
- **Magnetic Properties**: Genuine "W" quarters are non-magnetic due to their copper-nickel clad composition[7][9].  
- **Edge Reeding**: Each coin has 119 reeds; irregularities may indicate counterfeiting[5][7].

## Preservation and Care  

### Storage Best Practices  
- **Holders**: Use inert Mylar flips or NGC/PCGS slabs to prevent oxidation[14].  
- **Environment**: Store in low-humidity conditions (below 40% RH) at 18–22°C[12][14].  

### Handling Guidelines  
- Avoid touching the surface; handle by the edges with cotton gloves.  
- Clean only with distilled water and a soft microfiber cloth—never abrasive materials[12][14].
