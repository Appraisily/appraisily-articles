# The Comprehensive Guide to the 1982 Penny: Value, Rarity, and Historical Significance  

The 1982 Lincoln penny occupies a unique position in numismatic history due to its transitional composition and rare variants. While most 1982 pennies are worth face value, specific types—such as the **1982-D Small Date copper penny**—can command prices exceeding $18,000 at auction[6][9]. This guide explores the factors driving these valuations, the historical context of the coin’s production, and practical advice for collectors.  

---

## 1. What Is the 1982 Penny Worth, and Why Is It Important?  

The 1982 penny’s value hinges on its **composition**, **mint mark**, **date size**, and **errors**. Most 1982 pennies are common zinc-coated coins worth $0.01, but rare copper specimens or error coins can fetch four to five figures[2][6].  

### Key Reasons for Its Importance:  
- **Transitional Composition**: 1982 marked the shift from **95% copper** (pre-1982) to **97.5% zinc with a copper coating** (post-1982) to reduce production costs[8][13].  
- **Rarity of Copper Variants**: Copper pennies minted in 1982 are scarce, as the U.S. Mint phased them out mid-year. For example, the **1982-D Small Date copper penny** is one of the rarest modern coins, with only two confirmed specimens[9].  
- **Error Coins**: Double dies, off-center strikes, and transitional alloy errors (e.g., zinc pennies struck on copper planchets) add significant value[3][7].  

---

## 2. Historical Background and Development  

### The 1982 Composition Change  
Prior to 1982, pennies contained **95% copper**, but rising metal prices made this unsustainable. By January 1982, the U.S. Mint began producing **zinc-core pennies** (2.5% copper plating) to cut costs[8][13]. However, both compositions were minted concurrently, creating two distinct types:  
- **Copper Pennies**: Weigh **3.11 grams**, minted primarily in Philadelphia (no mint mark) and Denver (“D”)[6][9].  
- **Zinc Pennies**: Weigh **2.5 grams**, identifiable by their lighter feel and muted ring when dropped[3][8].  

### The Small Date vs. Large Date Variants  
Midway through 1982, the Mint adjusted the obverse die to create a **Small Date** design, characterized by a narrower “8” and “2” compared to the **Large Date**[5][6]. While most Small Date pennies are zinc, a handful were accidentally struck on copper planchets, creating ultra-rare collectibles[4][9].

## 4. Factors Determining Value  

### Critical Evaluation Criteria  
1. **Composition**: Copper pennies are inherently more valuable. Use a digital scale to confirm weight (3.11g = copper; 2.5g = zinc)[3][8].  
2. **Date Size**: Small Date variants are rarer, especially in copper[5][6].  
3. **Mint Mark**: Denver (“D”) produced the rarest copper Small Date pennies[4][9].  
4. **Condition**: Grading scales (e.g., PCGS/MS70) dramatically affect value. An MS67+ Red copper penny sold for **$9,987**[6].  
5. **Errors**: Double dies, repunched mint marks, and planchet flaws add premiums[3][7].

## 5. Identifying Authentic High-Value 1982 Pennies  

### Step-by-Step Authentication  
1. **Check the Date and Mint Mark**: Use a magnifier to identify Small Date features (slender “8”) and a “D” mint mark[1][4].  
2. **Weigh the Penny**: A 3.11g weight confirms copper composition[3][8].  
3. **Inspect for Errors**: Look for doubling, off-center strikes, or unusual coloration[7][13].  
4. **Assess Surface Quality**: Original red luster (RD) indicates higher grades, while brown (BN) tones suggest circulation[6][13].  

*Example*: A 1982-D Small Date penny weighing 3.11g with a sharp strike and red luster could be worth **$10,000+**[9].

## 8. Care and Preservation Tips  

### Best Practices  
- **Storage**: Use archival-quality holders (mylar flips, slabs) to prevent oxidation[12][14].  
- **Handling**: Wear cotton gloves and hold coins by the edges to avoid oils[11][12].  
- **Environment**: Maintain 35–45% humidity and 70°F (21°C) to prevent toning[12][14].  

### Cleaning Warnings  
- **Never Clean Copper**: Acids (vinegar, ketchup) strip patina and reduce value[11][14].  
- **Zinc Pennies**: Use distilled water and mild soap for surface dirt[11][14].
