## Historical Context and Development of RC Cola Bottles  

RC Cola’s lineage begins in 1905, when Claud Hatcher, a grocer in Cusseta, Georgia, founded Union Bottling Works after a dispute with a Coca-Cola syrup salesman[5]. His early ginger ale and Chero-Cola formulations evolved into Royal Crown Cola (renamed RC Cola in 1934), which became a Southern staple through partnerships with local bottlers[5]. The 1950s cemented its cultural relevance via the "RC Cola and Moon Pie" lunch combo, a working-class tradition that fueled regional brand loyalty[5].  

Glass bottle production mirrored technological advancements:  
- **1910s–1920s:** Hand-blown bottles with paper labels dominated, often bearing town-specific bottler names (e.g., "Nehi Beverage Co. Oregon"[3]).  
- **1930s–1940s:** Applied Color Label (ACL) technology enabled durable painted designs, such as the diamond logo on 1950s green glass bottles[3][15].  
- **1950s–1970s:** Commemorative editions emerged, like the 1974 Kentucky Derby bottle ($40[1]) and bicentennial pints ($18[11]).

### 1. **Base and Lip Codes**

### 1. **Cleaning Protocols**  
- **Interior:** Use lukewarm water, dish soap, and aquarium gravel for agitation, followed by alcohol rinses to prevent water spots[16][17].  
- **Exterior:** Avoid abrasive cleaners on ACL graphics; dab paper labels with distilled water[16].
