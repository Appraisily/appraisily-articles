## Historical Evolution of Coca-Cola Bottle Design and Value  

### Early Packaging Innovations (1890s-1915)  
The Coca-Cola Company's bottling history began with **Hutchinson-style bottles** (1894-1913), characterized by their straight sides and unique closure system using a rubber stopper and wire clamp. These bottles represent the holy grail for collectors, with intact examples like the 1899 Vicksburg, Mississippi bottle selling for $4,000-$5,000[1][4]. Only 12-15 distinct Hutchinson variants are documented, primarily from Southern bottlers in Alabama and Georgia[1][6].  

The transitional **straight-sided era (1900-1916)** saw bottlers using generic crown-top bottles until the iconic contour design emerged. These early 20th-century bottles show remarkable variation:  
- **Color**: Amber (Southern/Midwest, $400+) vs. clear/flint (national, $25-$150)[1]  
- **Embossing**: Shoulder-script vs. heel-script configurations affecting value by 300%[6]  
- **Capacity**: Rare 26-ounce "King Size" bottles (1910s) command $800-$1,200[4]
