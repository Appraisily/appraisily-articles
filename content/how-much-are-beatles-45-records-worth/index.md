# The Comprehensive Guide to the Value of Beatles 45 Records

The value of Beatles 45 records spans from a few dollars to over $125,000, depending on rarity, condition, and historical significance. ## Historical Background and Development of Beatles 45 Records  

### Early Pressings and Label Evolution  
Beatles 45s emerged in 1962 with Tony Sheridan’s *My Bonnie* on Decca, credited to "The Beat Brothers" in Canada[3]. By 1963, Capitol Records began distributing Beatles singles in North America, though initial pressings of *From Me to You* sold only 78 copies[3]. The transition to Apple Records in 1968 marked a shift toward artistic control, with singles like *Hey Jude* (Apple 2276) featuring iconic picture sleeves[1].  

### The Role of Regional Variations  
Canadian pressings, such as *She Loves You* (Capitol 72125), were often rushed to market using US metal parts, resulting in unique matrix numbers and limited quantities[3]. Meanwhile, UK releases on Parlophone prioritized mono mixes until 1967, creating distinct sonic profiles compared to stereo US counterparts[4].  

### Counterfeit Proliferation  
The 1964 album *Introducing The Beatles* became the most counterfeited record in history due to legal disputes between Vee-Jay and Capitol[18]. Fake copies often lack George Harrison’s shadow on the cover or feature misaligned labels[17][18].

## Authenticity Identification  

### Label and Cover Analysis  
- **"HONEY" Test**: Counterfeit *Introducing The Beatles* copies show printing flaws in the "H" and "E" of "HONEY" on the back cover[17][18].  
- **Spindle Hole Alignment**: Authentic US pressings place "The Beatles" above the spindle hole; counterfeits often misalign text[9][18].  

### Deadwax Etchings  
- **Machine-Stamped Codes**: Genuine copies include "Audio Matrix" or "MR" in runout grooves[17].  
- **Width**: Deadwax exceeding 22mm indicates counterfeit pressings[11].
