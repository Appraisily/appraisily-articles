## 1. The Concept and Importance of Painting Valuation  

Painting valuation is the process of determining the monetary, historical, and cultural worth of an artwork through systematic analysis. - **Insurance and Estate Planning**: Accurate valuations ensure adequate coverage and equitable inheritance distribution. The IRS mandates detailed appraisals for tax-deductible donations, requiring proof of authenticity and fair market value[3].

## 3. Categories of Painting Valuation  

### 3.1 Appraisal Contexts  
- **Fair Market Value**: Used for donations and estate taxes, reflecting a hypothetical buyer-seller agreement[3].  
- **Replacement Value**: Higher than FMV, covering insurance claims for rare items like Klimt’s *Portrait of Adele Bloch-Bauer I* ($135 million in 2006)[4].  
- **Liquidation Value**: Discounted prices for quick sales, often seen in bankruptcy auctions.  

### 3.2 Artistic Movements  
- **Old Masters**: Rarely traded; Leonardo da Vinci’s *Salvator Mundi* remains an outlier[4].  
- **Impressionist/Post-Impressionist**: Consistently high demand, with Monet’s *Haystacks* series averaging $110 million[13].  
- **Contemporary**: Volatile but lucrative; Jean-Michel Basquiat’s *Untitled* (1982) sold for $110.5 million in 2017[4].

## 4. Determinants of Painting Value  

### 4.1 Artist Reputation  
An artist’s market standing accounts for ~50% of a work’s value[5]. Living artists gain traction through biennials (e.g., Venice) and institutional acquisitions. For instance, Julie Mehretu’s *Hineni* (2018) appreciated 300% after a 2023 MoMA retrospective.  

### 4.2 Scarcity and Authenticity  
Limited editions and singular works command premiums. Only 15 authenticated Da Vinci paintings exist, justifying *Salvator Mundi*’s record price[15]. Conversely, Warhol’s mass-produced prints trade at 1/10th of his unique works[8].  

### 4.3 Provenance and Condition  
A documented lineage enhances value: Frida Kahlo’s *Diego y Yo* (1949) gained $30 million after tracing to Jacqueline Kennedy’s collection[14]. Restorations diminishing originality can reduce value by 20–40%[5].
