## 1. Defining Antique Postcard Value Websites  

Antique postcard value websites are digital platforms designed to assess, catalog, and facilitate the trade of vintage postcards. They serve as repositories of historical data, sales records, and appraisal tools, enabling users to determine the monetary and cultural worth of postcards produced primarily between the late 19th and mid-20th centuries. Their importance stems from three key functions:  

1. **Valuation Accuracy**: By aggregating sales data and expert appraisals, these sites mitigate the risk of undervaluing rare postcards. For instance, a 1915 Boston Red Sox postcard featuring Babe Ruth sold for $43,000, a figure discernible only through specialized platforms[4].  
2. **Market Accessibility**: They democratize access to niche markets, connecting sellers with global buyers. eBay, a prominent example, lists over 15,000 postcards at any given time, with rare examples like pre-1907 "undivided back" cards fetching premiums[5][8].  
3. **Historical Preservation**: By documenting postcards’ provenance and contextual significance, these platforms contribute to cultural heritage conservation. Real photo postcards (RPPCs) from the early 1900s, which capture vanishing urban landscapes, are particularly prized for their archival value[7][9].
