## 1. Fundamentals of Antique Knife Identification  
Antique knife identification involves authenticating age, origin, and craftsmanship through:  
- **Maker's marks analysis**: Symbols, logos, or signatures stamped on blades (95% of pre-1950 knives contain identifiable markings) [1][2]  
- **Material composition**: Distinguishing between wrought iron (pre-1800), carbon steel (19th century), and early stainless alloys (post-1913) [3][6]  
- **Construction techniques**: Identifying hand-forged vs. machine-stamped features through hammer marks and tang shaping [3][8]  

This process preserves cultural heritage and ensures accurate valuation, with properly identified 19th-century Bowie knives often appreciating 7-12% annually in collector markets [5][7].
