## Historical Evolution of Invicta’s Market Positioning  

### Founding and Early Innovations (1837–1970s)  
Founded in 1837 by Raphael Picard in La Chaux-de-Fonds, Switzerland, Invicta initially focused on manual-winding mechanical watches priced below luxury competitors[6]. The brand gained recognition for supplying naval officers in the USSR during the 1950s, emphasizing durability in harsh conditions[6]. However, the 1970s quartz crisis nearly obliterated Invicta, as electronic watches from Casio and Timex dominated the market[6].  

### Revival Under American Ownership (1991–Present)  
Acquired by a U.S. investment group in 1991, Invicta pivoted to automatic and quartz movements while expanding into pop-culture collaborations (e.g., Disney, Marvel)[6]. CEO Eyal Lalo’s leadership since 2004 catalyzed aggressive marketing, including TV shopping networks and limited editions like the Reddot Award-winning Subaqua Noma V[6][7]. This era saw Invicta transition from near-obscurity to a brand producing over 1,000 models annually, though critics argue this volume dilutes exclusivity[18].
