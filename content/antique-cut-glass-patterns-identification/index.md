## 2. Historical Evolution  
### Ancient Foundations (1500 BCE - 1600 CE)  
- Egyptian lapidary techniques: Diamond-point engraving used on core-formed vessels  
- Roman cage cups: 4th-century *diatreta* demonstrating advanced relief cutting  
- Islamic innovations: Floral arabesques cut into rock crystal, later adapted to glass[2]  

### Golden Age (1685-1820)  
- **George Ravenscroft's** 1676 lead glass formula enabled deeper cuts  
- English Regency patterns: Hobnail variations with 72-144 facets per square inch  
- Irish Waterford development: Characteristic flat diamond cuts with mitred edges  

### American Brilliant Period (1876-1917)  
Peak technical achievement featuring:  
- Russian pattern (crisscrossed fine lines)  
- Strawberry diamond (raised dot clusters)  
- Rosette medallions with 24+ petal elements[1]  
Over 5,000 documented patterns from this era[6]

## 8. Conservation Best Practices  
1. **Cleaning Protocol**:  
   - pH-neutral detergent (1 tsp to 1 gal water)  
   - 100% linen drying cloths  
   - Avoid ultrasonic cleaners - vibration damages delicate cuts  

2. **Display Requirements**:  
   - 40-50% humidity levels  
   - UV-filtered lighting (max 50 lux exposure)  
   - Felt-lined shelving with 1" clearance between pieces[7]  

3. **Restoration Ethics**:  
   - Never polish cut edges - removes original tool marks  
   - Epoxy repairs reduce value 90% - only use conservation-grade adhesives
