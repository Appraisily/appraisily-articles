## Historical Background and Development of the 1936 Wheat Penny  

### Origins of the Lincoln Wheat Cent Series  
The 1936 Wheat Penny belongs to the Lincoln cent series, first issued in 1909 to commemorate Abraham Lincoln’s 100th birthday. Designed by Victor David Brenner, it marked a historic shift as the first U.S. circulating coin to feature a real person’s portrait[3][4]. The reverse’s wheat stalks symbolized agricultural prosperity, earning the nickname "Wheat Penny." By 1936, the design had become a Depression-era staple, with over 309 million struck in Philadelphia alone[1][7].  

### Economic and Numismatic Context of 1936  
Minted during the Great Depression, the 1936 penny reflects broader economic struggles. Despite low copper prices, the U.S. Mint maintained the coin’s 95% copper composition, though many entered circulation and wore quickly[3][5]. San Francisco’s lower mintage (29.1 million) and Denver’s 40.6 million made these issues scarcer than Philadelphia’s 309.6 million, foreshadowing modern valuation disparities[2][7].

## Identifying Authentic 1936 Wheat Pennies  

### Key Authentication Features  
- **Weight and Diameter**: 3.11 grams and 19mm. Deviations suggest counterfeits[3][5].  
- **Mint Mark Placement**: “D” or “S” below the date on genuine coins; fakes often exhibit incorrect fonts or positions[1][7].  
- **Edge Inspection**: Plain edges distinguish Wheat Pennies from reeded-edge fakes[5].  

### Detecting Common Alterations  
- **Added Mint Marks**: Microscopic analysis reveals tooling marks or inconsistent patina[8].  
- **Artificial Toning**: Harsh chemical treatments create unnatural hues vs. gradual oxidation’s even layers[9].
