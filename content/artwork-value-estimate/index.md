## 1. The Fundamentals of Artwork Value Estimation  

### 1.1 Defining Artwork Value Estimation  
Artwork value estimation constitutes a professional assessment of an artwork’s monetary worth, informed by market data, provenance research, and condition analysis. Unlike static asset valuation, it reflects dynamic buyer-seller relationships and cultural zeitgeist shifts[2][9]. The process answers three primary valuation questions:  
- **Fair Market Value**: Price between willing parties without duress[7]  
- **Replacement Value**: Cost to acquire comparable work for insurance purposes[6]  
- **Liquidation Value**: Expected price under forced-sale conditions[5]  

A 2024 study of Sotheby’s auctions revealed 68% of contemporary art lots sold within 20% of their pre-sale estimates, demonstrating the market’s reliance on expert appraisals[12].  

### 1.2 Historical Significance and Modern Relevance
