Sotheby's free art appraisal is a preliminary valuation service providing non-binding estimates for items potentially suitable for auction or private sale through their global network. Unlike formal appraisals for insurance or tax purposes, this service focuses on market viability, offering clients insights into an item's auction potential without upfront costs[1][3].  

**Importance**:  
- **Accessibility**: Democratizes access to elite art market expertise, particularly for high-value items (e.g., paintings, jewelry)[2][8].  
- **Market Positioning**: Aligns with Sotheby's strategy to attract premium consignments by reducing barriers to entry[10][12].

- **1990s**: Digital databases improved comparative sales analysis, enhancing estimate precision[8].  

### 21st Century Digital Transformation  
- **2010**: Online submission portals reduced appraisal timelines from months to 15–20 business days[1][7].  
- **2019**: Machine learning integration began augmenting specialist assessments, particularly for mid-market items[8].
