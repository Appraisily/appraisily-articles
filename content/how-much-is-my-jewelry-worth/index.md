## Historical Evolution of Jewelry Valuation  

### Ancient Foundations to Renaissance Practices  
Jewelry appraisal originated in **3rd-century BCE Egypt**, where scribes documented gold purity and lapis lazuli quality in burial artifacts[10]. The Roman Empire institutionalized metal stamping with **imperial assay marks**, while medieval guilds developed early hallmarking systems to combat fraud[9]. The 1854 introduction of **karat standardization** in Britain marked the first modern precious metal grading system, later adopted globally through colonial trade networks[10].  

### 20th-Century Standardization  
The 1931 establishment of the **Gemological Institute of America (GIA)** revolutionized diamond grading through its **4Cs framework** (Cut, Color, Clarity, Carat)[9]. Post-WWII insurance demands led to the **ACORD 78/79 forms** (1968), creating uniform appraisal documentation requirements adopted by 92% of U.S. insurers[11]. The 1987 *Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice* (USPAP) mandated ethical guidelines still governing appraisers today[9].
