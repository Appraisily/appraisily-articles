## Introduction to the Number 13 Ball Mason Jar

The Number 13 Ball Mason jar is among the most sought-after pieces in the world of vintage canning jar collecting. These distinctive blue glass jars, marked with the number "13" on the bottom, have developed something of a mystique among collectors, with values often substantially higher than other vintage Ball Mason jars.

For both seasoned collectors and those who've just discovered an old jar in their grandmother's attic, understanding the value of these specific jars can be challenging. Prices vary widely depending on several key factors, including condition, color, and provenance. Let's dive into everything you need to know about the value and history of the Number 13 Ball Mason jar.

{{< content-modules/section-header title="History of the Number 13 Ball Mason Jar" level="2" >}}
The story behind these numbered jars adds to their collector appeal and explains their relative scarcity in today's market.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

Ball Mason jars with the number 13 were produced primarily between 1923 and 1933 by the Ball Corporation. During this era, the Ball Corporation used mold numbers on the bottom of their jars to track production and identify which mold was used to create each jar.

![Close-up of the number 13 marking on the bottom of a Ball Mason jar](https://ik.imagekit.io/appraisily/SEO/how-much-is-a-number-13-ball-mason-jar-worth/how-much-is-a-number-13-ball-mason-jar-worth-image-2_OjrDx5YbV.png)

Popular folklore suggests that due to superstition surrounding the number 13, many of these jars were destroyed or never purchased, making them rarer today. While manufacturers and historians dispute this claim, the legend persists and has contributed to their mystique and elevated value among collectors.

Several key aspects significantly impact the market value of these collectible jars, with condition being paramount.

When assessing the value of a Number 13 Ball Mason jar, consider these crucial factors:

1. **Condition** - Mint condition jars free of chips, cracks, and scratches command premium prices. Even minor damage can significantly decrease value.

2. **Color** - The blue aqua color is most desirable, with deeper, more vibrant blue hues generally worth more. Clear glass versions, while still collectible, typically fetch lower prices.

3. **Original Components** - Jars with original zinc lids and glass liners intact are worth considerably more than those without.

4. **Clarity and Glass Quality** - Glass quality, clarity, and the presence of bubbles or manufacturing quirks can either add to or detract from value, depending on collector preferences.

5. **Embossing Quality** - Crisp, clear embossing of the "Ball Perfect Mason" mark increases value.

{{< content-modules/section-header title="Tips for Collecting Number 13 Ball Mason Jars" level="2" >}}
For those interested in starting or expanding a collection of these historic jars, these guidelines can help make informed purchases.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

If you're venturing into collecting these vintage treasures, keep these expert tips in mind:

1. **Start with Education**: Learn to distinguish genuine Number 13 jars from regular Ball jars and reproductions. Resources like collector guides and YouTube videos can be invaluable.

2. **Inspect Before Buying**: Always examine jars carefully for damage, especially hairline cracks that may be difficult to see in photos when buying online.

3. **Check the Embossing**: Authentic Number 13 Ball Perfect Mason jars have clear, distinctive embossing. The number 13 on the bottom should be clearly legible.

4. **Consider Complete Sets**: Jars with original lids, particularly zinc lids with glass liners, are more valuable as complete items.

5. **Look Beyond eBay**: While eBay is a common source, estate sales, antique stores in rural areas, and regional auctions can yield finds at better prices.

6. **Connect with Other Collectors**: Join forums and collecting groups where you can share knowledge and potentially arrange private purchases or trades.

7. **Be Patient**: Building a quality collection takes time. Don't rush into purchases, especially for higher-priced examples.

8. **Document Your Collection**: Keep records of where and when you acquired each jar, along with any known history, which can add to their value over time.

## Conclusion: Is a Number 13 Ball Mason Jar a Worthy Investment?

The Number 13 Ball Mason jar represents a fascinating intersection of American manufacturing history, home canning culture, and collecting folklore. While values typically range from $30 to $200 depending on condition, color, and components, these jars offer more than just monetary value—they provide a tangible connection to America's past.

For collectors, the Number 13 Ball Mason jar remains an accessible entry point into antique glass collecting. Unlike some antiques that command thousands of dollars, most Number 13 jars can be acquired for reasonable sums, making them appealing to both serious collectors and casual enthusiasts.

Whether you've discovered one in a family collection or are considering adding one to your display cabinet, the Number 13 Ball Mason jar carries with it a rich history and the mystique of its supposedly "unlucky" number—which, ironically, has made these jars quite lucky indeed for those who possess them.

Remember that condition is paramount in determining value, and authentication is important when making significant purchases. By understanding what makes these jars special and following the guidelines in this article, you'll be well-equipped to assess the true value of a Number 13 Ball Mason jar in today's collector market.
