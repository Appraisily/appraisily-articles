### Types of Antique Furniture Appraisals

Different circumstances call for different types of appraisals:

1. **Insurance Appraisal**: Determines replacement value for insurance coverage.
2. **Estate Appraisal**: Establishes fair market value for estate settlements or tax purposes.
3. **Donation Appraisal**: Documents value for charitable contribution tax deductions.
4. **Resale Appraisal**: Assesses likely selling price in current market conditions.
5. **Verbal Consultation**: Informal assessment without formal documentation.

### The Appraisal Process

A professional appraisal typically follows these steps:

1. **Initial Consultation**: Discuss your needs and the appraiser explains their process and fees
2. **Examination**: The appraiser inspects your furniture in person, taking photographs and notes
3. **Research**: The appraiser researches comparable items, sales records, and market conditions
4. **Documentation**: A formal appraisal report is prepared with detailed descriptions and valuations
5. **Delivery**: The final report is provided, often with an opportunity to ask questions

### Documentation to Gather

Collect any information you have about your furniture pieces:

- **Provenance documents**: Previous appraisals, bills of sale, inheritance letters
- **Historical information**: Family stories, known history of the piece
- **Restoration records**: Documentation of any repairs or refinishing
- **Photographs**: Historical photos showing the piece in previous settings

### Furniture Preparation

Prepare your furniture properly:

1. **Clean gently**: Remove dust and debris without using harsh chemicals
2. **Make accessible**: Ensure the appraiser can examine all parts of the furniture
3. **Don't restore**: Allow the professional to see the piece in its current state
4. **Group related items**: Keep furniture sets or related pieces together

### Questions to Ask Before Booking

When contacting potential appraisers, ask these important questions:

1. What are your qualifications and specializations?
2. Are you USPAP compliant and affiliated with professional associations?
3. What types of appraisals do you provide, and which is appropriate for my needs?
4. What are your fees and payment structure?
5. Will you provide a written report, and what will it include?
6. How long will the appraisal process take?
7. Do you have references I can contact?

### In-Person Appraisals: The Gold Standard

Traditional in-person appraisals remain the most comprehensive option:

**Advantages:**
- Allows thorough physical examination of construction, materials, and condition
- Enables detection of repairs, alterations, and authenticity markers
- Provides opportunity for direct discussion and questions
- Best option for high-value or complex pieces

**Disadvantages:**
- Generally more expensive than online options
- May require transportation of furniture or appraiser travel fees
- Scheduling constraints may cause delays

### Online Appraisal Services: Convenient Alternatives

Online appraisal services like [Value My Stuff](https://www.valuemystuff.com/us/categories/furniture-design/antique-furniture) offer faster, more affordable options:

**Advantages:**
- Lower cost than in-person appraisals
- Quick turnaround (often 24-48 hours)
- No need to transport furniture
- Convenience of digital communication

**Disadvantages:**
- Relies solely on photographs and descriptions
- May miss subtle condition issues or construction details
- Limited interaction with the appraiser
- Less suitable for very valuable or complicated pieces

According to Value My Stuff, you can "Get your antiques valued online by one of our Value My Stuff Furniture Experts! Check our pricing and buy an appraisal package that suits you best!"

### Making the Right Choice

Consider these factors when deciding between online and in-person appraisals:

1. **Value and importance**: Higher-value items typically warrant in-person expertise
2. **Urgency**: Online appraisals generally provide faster results
3. **Budget**: In-person appraisals cost more but offer greater thoroughness
4. **Purpose**: Insurance and estate appraisals may require in-person verification
5. **Mobility**: Large or fragile pieces may be difficult to transport

Many appraisers now offer hybrid services, starting with online consultation followed by in-person examination if warranted.
