Emmett Kelly (1898-1979) was one of America's most beloved circus performers, best known for his creation of the "Weary Willie" character—a sad-faced hobo clown based on Depression-era tramps. His distinctive persona made him a cultural icon, appearing with Ringling Bros. and Barnum & Bailey Circus, in films, and even on television.

### Manufacturer and Series

The company that produced the figurine significantly impacts its value. Different manufacturers have created Emmett Kelly figurines over the years, with varying quality and collectibility:

- **Flambro Collection**: Among the most common Emmett Kelly Jr. figurines
- **The Original Emmett Kelly Circus Collection**: Often more valuable than later reproductions
- **Royal Doulton**: Known for higher quality materials and craftsmanship
- **Limited Edition Collections**: Typically command premium prices when authenticated

According to collectibles experts, many figurines labeled "Emmett Kelly Jr." were mass-produced by companies like Flambro, while original Emmett Kelly pieces are typically older and may hold greater value.

### Key Identification Markers

When examining your figurines, look for these identifying features:

1. **Base markings** - Check for manufacturer name, copyright date, and edition information
2. **Signatures** - Original signatures will show variation in pressure and ink flow, while reproduced signatures appear uniform
3. **Material quality** - Higher-value pieces typically use finer porcelain or ceramic with detailed hand-painting
4. **Size and weight** - Premium figurines often have substantial weight and precise detailing
5. **Design accuracy** - Authentic pieces faithfully capture Kelly's famous "Weary Willie" character

### Avoiding Reproductions and Fakes

The popularity of Emmett Kelly figurines has led to unauthorized reproductions. Here's how to spot potential fakes:

- **Poor paint application** - Uneven or sloppy detailing, especially on the face
- **Lightweight materials** - Authentic figurines typically have substantial weight
- **Missing or suspicious markings** - Vague or inconsistent base stamps
- **Artificially aged** - Signs of intentional distressing to make new pieces look vintage
- **Documentation inconsistencies** - Certificates with printing errors or historical inaccuracies

According to collector forums, reputable manufacturers like Flambro, Royal Doulton, and licensed producers of "The Original Emmett Kelly Circus Collection" have consistent quality standards that help distinguish authentic pieces.

### Display and Storage Tips

1. **Avoid direct sunlight** - UV rays can fade paint and damage delicate materials
2. **Use stable, level surfaces** - Prevent accidental falls or stress on delicate parts
3. **Display cases with glass or acrylic fronts** - Protect from dust while allowing visibility
4. **Control humidity** - Maintain 40-50% relative humidity to prevent material degradation
5. **Handle with clean hands** - Natural oils can damage paint and finishes over time

### Cleaning and Maintenance

Proper cleaning techniques will preserve your figurines without causing damage:

1. **Dust with a soft, natural-bristle brush** - Never use stiff bristles or abrasive materials
2. **Avoid liquid cleaners when possible** - If necessary, use a slightly damp microfiber cloth
3. **Never immerse in water** - This can damage adhesives and paint
4. **Professional restoration only** - Amateur repairs can significantly decrease value
5. **Document condition regularly** - Keep photos and condition notes for insurance and value tracking

Maintaining original boxes, certificates, and documentation in acid-free storage will also help preserve the overall value of your collection.
