## Introduction to the 1904 Indian Head Penny

The 1904 Indian Head penny represents one of the final issues of this iconic American coin series before it was replaced by the Lincoln cent in 1909. With its distinctive design featuring Liberty wearing a Native American headdress, these pennies have become cherished collectibles for numismatists and history enthusiasts alike.

If you've recently discovered a 1904 Indian Head penny in your collection, at a garage sale, or perhaps in an old family heirloom, you might be wondering about its value. The good news is that all 1904 Indian Head pennies hold at least some premium over face value, but the exact worth depends on several key factors that we'll explore throughout this comprehensive guide.

{{< content-modules/section-header title="Historical Background and Design Features" level="2" >}}

The Indian Head penny series was designed by James Barton Longacre, Chief Engraver of the United States Mint, and was produced from 1859 to 1909. Despite its name, the coin doesn't actually depict a Native American but rather Lady Liberty wearing a Native American headdress – a design choice that was common in 19th-century American iconography.

![Close-up view of the 1904 Indian Head Penny showing detailed design features](https://ik.imagekit.io/appraisily/SEO/how-much-is-a-1904-indian-head-penny-worth/how-much-is-a-1904-indian-head-penny-worth-image-2_Fa7KFb0hi.png)

### Design Specifications:

- **Obverse (Front)**: Features Liberty wearing a Native American headdress with the word "LIBERTY" on the headband. The design is surrounded by the text "UNITED STATES OF AMERICA" and the date "1904" at the bottom.
- **Reverse (Back)**: Displays a laurel wreath (oak wreath after 1860) encircling the denomination "ONE CENT."
- **Composition**: 95% copper, 5% tin and zinc
- **Diameter**: 19 mm
- **Weight**: 3.11 grams
- **Edge**: Plain

### Mintage and Rarity:

The Philadelphia Mint produced 61,328,284 Indian Head pennies in 1904 for general circulation, a relatively high mintage for the series. Additionally, 1,817 proof versions were struck specifically for collectors.

While the 1904 Indian Head penny isn't considered rare in the series, finding examples in high-grade uncirculated condition or with error varieties can be challenging, which significantly affects their value.

### Color Designation

For copper coins like the Indian Head penny, color plays a crucial role in grading and valuation:

1. **Red (RD)**: Coins retaining at least 95% of their original mint luster and copper color. These command the highest premiums.
2. **Red-Brown (RB)**: Coins showing a mix of original red color and brown toning (40-94% red).
3. **Brown (BN)**: Coins that have turned mostly brown due to oxidation (less than 40% red remaining).

Most circulated 1904 Indian Head pennies will have turned brown, while uncirculated examples might retain some of their original red or red-brown color, significantly enhancing their value.

### Error Varieties and Die Variations

Certain error varieties and die variations can make your 1904 Indian Head penny much more valuable:

1. **Repunched Date (RPD)**: Some 1904 pennies show evidence of the date being punched multiple times in slightly different positions. You can notice doubling on the numbers 9, 0, and/or 4.

2. **Die Cracks and Cuds**: Late-state die cracks or cuds (raised, irregular blobs of metal usually on the coin's rim) can add premiums to otherwise common dates.

3. **Off-Center Strikes**: Coins struck off-center by 5% or more can be worth significantly more than perfectly centered examples.

{{< content-modules/section-header title="1904 Proof Indian Head Pennies" level="2" >}}
  Proof coins represent the pinnacle of the mint's craftsmanship and are struck specifically for collectors.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

The Philadelphia Mint produced 1,817 proof Indian Head pennies in 1904. These special collector editions were struck using polished dies and planchets, resulting in coins with mirror-like fields and frosted devices (known as "cameo" contrast).

Proof specimens, particularly those with strong cameo contrast or deep mirror-like fields, command significant premiums. According to recent auction data, a 1904 PR 66 penny is worth approximately $1,500, while those graded PR 67 can fetch around $5,500, especially with CAC certification (Certified Acceptance Corporation – an independent quality verification service).

### Authentication Checklist:

1. **Weight and Size**: Authentic 1904 Indian Head pennies should weigh approximately 3.11 grams and measure 19mm in diameter.

2. **Design Details**: Study the fine details of Liberty's face, the feathers in the headdress, and the lettering. Counterfeits often lack the sharpness and precision of genuine mint products.

3. **Edge Examination**: The edge should be plain (not reeded) and appear natural. Be wary of filed edges, which might indicate alterations.

4. **Date and Mint Mark**: The 1904 Indian Head penny was only produced at the Philadelphia Mint and has no mint mark. Be suspicious of any 1904 Indian Head penny claiming to be from another mint.

5. **Magnetic Test**: The 1904 Indian Head penny is made of bronze (95% copper, 5% tin and zinc) and should not be magnetic. If your coin sticks to a magnet, it's likely counterfeit.

For valuable specimens or if you're considering a significant purchase, professional third-party grading from services like PCGS (Professional Coin Grading Service) or NGC (Numismatic Guaranty Corporation) is recommended.

### Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
- Handle your coin by the edges only
- Use cotton gloves when possible
- Store in archival-quality holders, flips, or albums
- Keep in a stable environment with controlled temperature and humidity
- Have valuable specimens professionally graded and encapsulated

**DON'T:**
- Clean your coins (this can significantly reduce their value)
- Touch the surfaces with bare fingers
- Store in leather, PVC plastic, or paper envelopes
- Expose to extreme temperature fluctuations
- Use abrasive materials or chemicals

The 1904 Indian Head penny falls into the category of more common dates within the series. It doesn't command the premium of key dates like the 1877 (with just 852,500 minted) or the 1909-S (the only Indian Head penny produced at the San Francisco Mint and the final year of production).

However, the 1904 issue still represents an important part of American numismatic history and a tangible connection to the early 20th century. As the series grows more distant in time, even the more common dates like 1904 continue to appreciate in value, particularly in higher grades.

## Conclusion: Is Your 1904 Indian Head Penny a Hidden Treasure?

While most 1904 Indian Head pennies in circulation condition are worth a modest premium over face value ($2-15 depending on condition), certain specimens can be significantly more valuable. High-grade uncirculated examples, particularly those retaining their original red color, can command prices from $65 to $200 or more. For the rare proof specimens, values can exceed $5,000 for the finest examples.

The historical significance of the Indian Head penny series, combined with its beautiful and distinctly American design, ensures that these coins will continue to be sought after by collectors. Whether your 1904 Indian Head penny is worth a few dollars or a few thousand, it represents an important piece of American numismatic heritage worth preserving.

If you believe you have a valuable specimen, consider consulting with a professional numismatist or submitting it to a third-party grading service for authentication and valuation. And remember, even if your coin isn't worth a fortune, the stories it could tell about its 119-year journey make it a treasure of history in its own right.
