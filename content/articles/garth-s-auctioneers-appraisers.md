## Introduction to Garth's Auctioneers & Appraisers

Garth's Auctioneers & Appraisers stands as one of America's most respected auction houses, with a rich history dating back to 1954. Founded by Garth Oberlander, this Ohio-based institution has become a cornerstone in the antiques and fine art market, especially in the Midwest region. Whether you're looking to buy, sell, or simply understand the value of your treasured items, Garth's offers expertise across a wide range of collectibles and antiques.

{{< content-modules/section-header title="History and Legacy of Garth's Auctions" level="2" >}}
With nearly seven decades of experience, Garth's has established itself as Ohio's oldest auction house, building a reputation for integrity, expertise, and exceptional service.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

Founded in 1954 by Garth Oberlander, the auction house has grown from humble beginnings to become a national force in the world of antiques and fine art. Throughout its history, Garth's has maintained a commitment to exceptional service and expertise, adapting to changing markets while preserving traditional auction values.

As the oldest auction house in Ohio, Garth's has handled countless significant collections and estates, building relationships with collectors, museums, and institutions across the country. The company's longevity speaks to its adaptability and consistent quality of service over the decades.

{{< content-modules/section-header title="Auction Services and Process" level="2" >}}
Understanding Garth's auction process can help both buyers and sellers navigate the world of auction sales more effectively.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

Garth's conducts dozens of auctions annually, offering both traditional in-person events and online bidding options. Their comprehensive auction services cover everything from initial evaluation to final settlement.

### For Sellers (Consignors)

The consignment process at Garth's typically follows these steps:

1. **Initial Consultation**: Prospective consignors can submit photos and information about their items online, by phone, or in person.

2. **Professional Evaluation**: Garth's specialists will assess items for authenticity, condition, and market value.

3. **Consignment Agreement**: Once accepted, sellers sign an agreement detailing commission rates, reserves (if applicable), and other terms.

4. **Catalog Preparation**: Items are photographed, researched, and described for inclusion in auction catalogs.

5. **Marketing**: Garth's promotes upcoming auctions through multiple channels to reach qualified buyers.

6. **Auction Event**: Items are sold to the highest bidder, either in person or online.

7. **Settlement**: Approximately 30-45 days after the auction, sellers receive payment for sold items (minus agreed commission).

### For Buyers

Participating in Garth's auctions as a buyer involves:

1. **Registration**: Create an account online or register in person before the auction.

2. **Preview**: View items either online or during in-person preview days.

3. **Bidding Options**: Bid in person at the auction, place absentee bids, bid by phone, or participate online through platforms like Invaluable.

4. **Payment & Pickup**: Successful bidders pay for and collect their items according to the terms specified for each auction.

{{< content-modules/section-header title="Appraisal Services" level="2" >}}
Beyond auctions, Garth's offers professional appraisal services for various purposes, from insurance and estate planning to tax considerations and donation.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

Garth's team includes multiple certified appraisers with specialized knowledge across diverse collecting categories. Their appraisal services adhere to industry standards and are accepted by major insurance companies, the IRS, and courts.

### Types of Appraisals Offered

1. **Insurance Appraisals**: Documents the replacement value of items for insurance coverage purposes.

2. **Estate Appraisals**: Determines fair market value for estate tax filing and division of assets.

3. **Charitable Donation Appraisals**: Establishes value for tax deductions on donated items.

4. **Damage/Loss Appraisals**: Assesses value of damaged or lost items for insurance claims.

5. **Pre-Sale Evaluations**: Estimates potential auction values for those considering selling.

Appraisals can be conducted either on-site (for large collections or estates) or by appointment at Garth's facilities. Their written appraisals comply with Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice (USPAP) requirements.

{{< content-modules/section-header title="Tips for Working with Garth's" level="2" >}}
Whether you're buying, selling, or seeking appraisal services, these practical tips can help you make the most of your experience with Garth's.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

### For Sellers

1. **Research Before Consigning**: Understand the general value range of your items before approaching any auction house.

2. **Ask About Marketing Plans**: Inquire about how Garth's will promote your specific items or collection.

3. **Understand All Fees**: Besides the commission, ask about photography, insurance, storage, and unsold lot fees.

4. **Consider Timing**: Some categories sell better at certain times of the year; discuss optimal scheduling.

5. **Maintain Realistic Expectations**: Market values fluctuate; be prepared for results that may differ from estimates.

### For Buyers

1. **Preview Thoroughly**: Whenever possible, examine items in person during preview days.

2. **Research Comparable Sales**: Look at past auction results for similar items to establish value ranges.

3. **Set Firm Budgets**: Decide your maximum bid before the auction to avoid emotional overspending.

4. **Factor in Additional Costs**: Remember to account for buyer's premium (typically 20-25%), sales tax, shipping, and insurance.

5. **Build Relationships**: Introducing yourself to specialists can provide valuable insights and opportunities.

### For Appraisal Clients

1. **Organize Information**: Gather any documentation, provenance, or purchase records before your appointment.

2. **Be Specific About Purpose**: Different appraisal types serve different purposes with varying methodologies.

3. **Ask About Credentials**: Confirm that the appraiser assigned to your items has relevant expertise in that category.

4. **Get Clear Cost Estimates**: Request a written estimate of appraisal fees before proceeding.

5. **Update Regularly**: Insurance appraisals should be updated every 3-5 years as market values change.
