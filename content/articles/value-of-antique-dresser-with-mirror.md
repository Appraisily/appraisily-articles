### Materials and Construction

The type of wood used in an antique dresser significantly affects its value. High-quality hardwoods like mahogany, walnut, and oak typically command higher prices than softer woods like pine. Additionally, dressers featuring exotic woods, burl veneers, or marble tops generally have greater value.

Construction quality is equally important. Pieces with dovetail joints, hand-carved details, and solid wood construction (rather than veneer over lesser materials) are more valuable. According to [Mearto's antique dresser appraisal guide](https://www.mearto.com/categories/dressers), handcrafted details significantly elevate a piece's worth.

{{< content-modules/section-header title="How to Identify Your Antique Dresser with Mirror" level="2" >}}
Proper identification is crucial to determining an accurate value for your antique dresser with mirror. By examining specific characteristics, you can narrow down the period and style of your piece.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

### Examining Construction Methods

One of the most reliable ways to determine the age of an antique dresser is by examining its construction methods. According to [Old Plank's guide on dating antique furniture](https://oldplank.com/blog/how-to-determine-the-age-of-antique-furniture/), you should:

1. Look at the joinery: Hand-cut dovetail joints indicate pre-1860s construction, while machine-cut dovetails suggest later manufacturing.
2. Check the drawer construction: Earlier pieces often have wooden drawer guides, while later ones may have center wooden or metal guides.
3. Examine the back and bottom: Earlier pieces typically have irregularly sized wooden panels, while later ones might have plywood or more uniform construction.

### Mirror Characteristics

The mirror itself provides important clues to a dresser's age and authenticity:

- Pre-1850s mirrors often show significant silvering deterioration or have been replaced
- Victorian-era mirrors typically feature oval or rectangular shapes with ornate frames
- Art Deco mirrors tend to have simpler, geometric framing
- Original mirrors add significant value to the piece

According to [Reperch's guide on antique mirror valuation](https://reperch.com/blog/how-to-identify-your-antique-mirrors-value-our-guide), some signs of aging like clouding from mercury oxidation can actually increase a mirror's desirability to collectors, while significant damage decreases value.

### Hardware Examination

Original hardware provides valuable clues about a dresser's age:

- Pre-1850s: Often featured cast brass pulls with backplates
- Victorian Era: Ornate brass pulls, sometimes with porcelain inserts
- Eastlake Period: Geometric design pulls with angular details
- Art Nouveau: Flowing, organic hardware designs
- Art Deco: Sleek, often elongated metal pulls

Replacement hardware can significantly reduce a dresser's value, so examine pulls and hinges carefully for consistency with the piece's apparent period.

{{< content-modules/section-header title="Restoration and Value Considerations" level="2" >}}
The decision to restore an antique dresser with mirror involves careful consideration of how potential improvements might affect the piece's value and authenticity.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

### When Restoration Enhances Value

In some cases, professional restoration can increase a dresser's value:

- Stabilizing structural issues that threaten the piece's longevity
- Carefully repairing damaged veneer using period-appropriate techniques
- Professional restoration of the mirror's silvering when severely deteriorated
- Refinishing only when the current finish is badly damaged and using period-appropriate methods

### When Restoration Diminishes Value

Many restoration efforts can actually decrease a dresser's value:

- Complete refinishing that removes the patina and original finish
- Replacing original hardware with modern reproductions
- Adding non-period decorative elements
- Using modern materials or techniques incompatible with the piece's age
- Extensive modifications that alter the original design

Collectors generally prefer pieces with original finishes and hardware, even with some wear, over heavily restored examples. According to antique experts, preserving the original integrity is often more valuable than achieving a "like new" appearance.

### Finding Qualified Restoration Professionals

If restoration is necessary, work with professionals who specialize in antique furniture and understand period-appropriate techniques. Ask for:

- Examples of previous work on similar pieces
- Their approach to preserving original materials
- References from collectors or dealers
- Their knowledge of the specific period of your dresser

Proper documentation of any restoration work can help maintain the piece's provenance and value for future owners.
