Christian Riese Lassen, born in 1949, is a Hawaiian-based artist who has gained international recognition for his striking, color-saturated marine paintings. Originally from California, Lassen moved to Maui in the 1970s where the island's natural beauty profoundly influenced his artistic development.

![Christian Riese Lassen at work in his studio creating vibrant marine art](https://ik.imagekit.io/appraisily/SEO/christian-lassen-art-appraisal/christian-lassen-art-appraisal-image-2_T94dc3Fw9.png)

Lassen's signature style combines elements of realism and fantasy, using acrylics, enamels, and oils to create luminous underwater scenes. His work often features dolphins, whales, and tropical fish set against vibrant coral reefs. Beyond his artistic achievements, Lassen is known for his environmental activism, using his art to raise awareness about ocean conservation.

When appraising Christian Riese Lassen's artwork, several key factors determine its market value:

### Medium and Format

Lassen has worked with various media throughout his career. Original acrylic or oil paintings on canvas command the highest prices, typically ranging from $1,500 to over $10,000 depending on size, subject matter, and condition. Limited edition prints and reproductions, including his popular "Lassengraphs," are more accessible but vary significantly in value.

### Authenticity and Signature

Genuine Lassen artwork features his distinctive signature, typically in the lower corner of the piece. Authentic works often include certificates of authenticity from authorized dealers or publishers. For limited editions, proper numbering (e.g., 150/750) should be present alongside the signature.

![Close-up of a Christian Riese Lassen signature and limited edition numbering on artwork](https://ik.imagekit.io/appraisily/SEO/christian-lassen-art-appraisal/christian-lassen-art-appraisal-image-3_cd2kVvpyQ.png)

### Size and Composition

Larger pieces and those featuring Lassen's most iconic subjects (dolphins, whales, underwater scenes with vibrant colors) typically command higher prices. Particularly sought-after are pieces with dramatic lighting effects and complex compositions.

### Condition

The physical condition significantly impacts value. Issues such as fading, scratches, water damage, or improper framing can substantially reduce a piece's worth. For canvas works, tears or professional restorations should be noted in any appraisal.

### Provenance and Documentation

Documented history of ownership adds value, particularly for original paintings. Original purchase receipts, certificates of authenticity, and gallery documentation all enhance an artwork's provenance and, consequently, its value.

### Rarity and Edition Size

For limited editions, smaller edition sizes typically command higher prices. Special releases, artist proofs (marked A/P), and rare collaborative works (such as his Disney collaborations) often have premium values in the collector market.

### Historical Significance

Pieces from significant periods in the artist's career or those featured in major exhibitions may have additional historical value. Early works from the 1970s and 1980s, before his commercial expansion, are particularly sought after by serious collectors.

### Original Paintings

Lassen's original acrylic and oil paintings represent his highest-value works. These one-of-a-kind pieces feature his distinctive technique of layering colors to create luminous underwater scenes. At auction, original Lassen canvases have sold for between $1,500 to $10,000, with exceptional pieces occasionally exceeding this range.

According to data from [Invaluable.com](https://www.invaluable.com/artist/lassen-christian-riese-wl5iwkmppe/sold-at-auction-prices/), factors that particularly impact original painting values include:

- Canvas size (larger works command premium prices)
- Subject matter (iconic dolphin scenes are most valuable)
- Era (1980s paintings often sell for higher prices)
- Condition (pristine examples without restoration sell best)

### Limited Edition Prints and Lassengraphs

Lassen has produced numerous limited edition prints throughout his career. His "Lassengraphs" are a proprietary printing process that recreates the dimensional quality and color vibrancy of his original paintings.

Limited edition values vary based on:
- Edition size (smaller runs are more valuable)
- Numbering (lower numbers generally preferred)
- Special designations (artist proofs marked "A/P" command premiums)
- Subject popularity (dolphins and whales are most sought-after)

### Disney Collaborations

A unique subset of Lassen's work includes his Disney character collaborations, where he incorporated iconic Disney characters into his marine environments. These specialized pieces, particularly limited editions, have a dedicated collector market with values ranging from $200 to $1,500 depending on rarity, condition, and the characters featured.

### Artagraphs and Textured Reproductions

Artagraphs are dimensional oil on canvas reproductions that recreate both the color and surface texture of Lassen's original paintings. As noted in an [eBay listing](https://www.ebay.com/itm/153602357777), these pieces offer a more affordable way to collect Lassen's work while still maintaining some of the textural qualities of originals. Values typically range from $100 to $600.

Authenticity is paramount when appraising Lassen's work, as unauthorized reproductions exist in the market. Here's how to verify the authenticity of a Lassen piece:

### Signature Analysis

Lassen's signature is distinctive and consistent. Authentic pieces feature his signature, typically in the lower right or left corner. The signature should appear fluid and natural, not mechanical or stamped. On limited editions, look for hand-signed pieces rather than printed signatures.

### Certificate of Authenticity

Legitimate Lassen artwork, particularly limited editions and prints, should come with proper documentation. Certificates of authenticity (COAs) from authorized dealers like Lassen Art or recognized galleries provide crucial verification. These certificates typically include:

- Title of the artwork
- Medium and dimensions
- Edition number (if applicable)
- Date of creation
- Artist's signature
- Publisher or gallery information

### Edition Verification

For limited editions, proper numbering should be present (e.g., "150/750"). Verify that the numbering matches what's documented on the certificate of authenticity. Artist proofs are marked "A/P" instead of a number.

### Physical Examination

Examining the artwork under proper lighting can reveal important authentication clues:

- **For Original Paintings**: Look for evidence of brushwork, texture, and layering characteristic of Lassen's technique
- **For Limited Editions**: Examine the quality of printing, paper, and inks
- **For Lassengraphs**: Check for the distinctive dimensional quality that sets them apart from standard prints

### Copyright Information

Authentic Lassen works often include copyright information, either on the artwork itself or accompanying documentation. This typically appears as "© Christian Riese Lassen" followed by the year.

### Publisher Verification

Contacting the original publisher or [Lassen Art](https://www.lassenart.com/) directly can help verify authenticity. According to their contact page, while they don't provide appraisals themselves, they can verify if a piece was published through official channels.

![Example of Christian Riese Lassen artwork authentication elements including signature and certificate](https://ik.imagekit.io/appraisily/SEO/christian-lassen-art-appraisal/christian-lassen-art-appraisal-image-5_Z0lUoCRWp.png)

### Red Flags for Potential Forgeries

Be wary of these warning signs that may indicate an inauthentic Lassen work:

- Missing or inconsistent signature
- Lack of proper documentation
- Unusually low price compared to market value
- Poor print quality or colors that don't match Lassen's vibrant palette
- Seller's unwillingness to provide provenance information
- Edition numbers that don't match documentation

If you're uncertain about authenticity, professional appraisers specializing in contemporary art can provide expert verification before purchasing or selling a piece.

If you own Christian Riese Lassen artwork and need a professional valuation, here's what you should know about the appraisal process:

### When to Seek Professional Appraisal

Consider getting a professional appraisal in these situations:

- Insurance documentation and coverage
- Estate planning and tax purposes
- Divorce or asset division
- Donation to charitable organizations
- Preparation for sale
- Authentication concerns

### Types of Art Appraisals

Different situations require different types of appraisals:

1. **Insurance Appraisal**: Determines replacement value for insurance coverage
2. **Fair Market Value Appraisal**: Establishes the likely price between willing buyer and seller (used for tax purposes, donations)
3. **Liquidation Value**: Determines value in forced or time-constrained sales
4. **Authentication Assessment**: Focuses on verifying the work's authenticity

### Finding Qualified Appraisers

Since Lassen Art itself doesn't provide appraisals, they recommend contacting the [Appraiser Association of America](https://www.appraisersassociation.org/) at (212) 889-5404, as noted on their [contact page](https://www.lassenart.com/contact-us/).

When selecting an appraiser, look for these qualifications:

- Membership in professional organizations like the American Society of Appraisers (ASA) or the International Society of Appraisers (ISA)
- Specialization in contemporary art, particularly marine art or limited editions
- Experience with Lassen's work specifically
- Adherence to the Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice (USPAP)

### Appraisal Costs

According to industry standards, professional art appraisals typically cost between $150-$500 per hour for a USPAP-compliant evaluation. Factors affecting the cost include:

- Type of appraisal needed
- Number of pieces being appraised
- Complexity of research required
- Appraiser's expertise and credentials
- Geographic location

### Preparing for an Appraisal

To maximize the value and accuracy of your appraisal, prepare:

1. **Documentation**: Gather all available paperwork related to the artwork, including:
   - Original purchase receipts
   - Certificates of authenticity
   - Gallery documentation
   - Previous appraisals
   - Exhibition history

2. **Condition Assessment**: Note any damage, repairs, or restoration work

3. **Provenance Information**: Document the history of ownership

4. **Photography**: Take clear photographs of the front, back, signature, edition numbers, and any distinguishing features

5. **Measurements**: Record accurate dimensions including frame (if applicable)

### Online vs. In-Person Appraisals

While in-person appraisals are generally more comprehensive, reputable online appraisal services like [JustAnswer](https://www.justanswer.com/appraisals/qlb8f-possibly-christian-lassen-painting-perfect.html) can provide preliminary valuations based on photographs and documentation. These services are typically more affordable but may not meet requirements for insurance or tax purposes.

{{< content-modules/faq title="FAQs About Christian Lassen Art" id="faqs-about-christian-lassen-art" >}}
  {{< content-modules/faq-item question="How can I tell if my Christian Lassen artwork is an original or a print?" >}}
    Original Christian Lassen paintings will show clear brushwork, texture, and layering of paint when examined closely. Look for subtle impasto and variation in the paint surface. Prints, including limited editions, will have a flat surface with uniform dot patterns visible under magnification. Original works are hand-signed, while some prints have printed signatures. Originals are typically acrylic or oil on canvas, while prints are on paper or sometimes canvas for giclées. When in doubt, consult with a professional appraiser who specializes in contemporary art.
  {{< /content-modules/faq-item >}}
  
  {{< content-modules/faq-item question="What is a Lassengraph and how does it differ from a regular print?" >}}
    A Lassengraph is a proprietary printing process developed specifically for reproducing Christian Riese Lassen's artwork. Unlike standard lithographic or giclée prints, Lassengraphs use specialized techniques to recreate the dimensional quality and vibrant colors of Lassen's original paintings. They feature enhanced texture and depth that standard prints lack. Lassengraphs are typically produced in limited editions, hand-signed by the artist, and come with certificates of authenticity. Their value is generally higher than regular open-edition prints but lower than original paintings.
