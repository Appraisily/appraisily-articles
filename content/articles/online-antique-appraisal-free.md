### Providing Detailed Descriptions

Along with quality photographs, a thorough description helps appraisers understand your item's history and context. Include:

1. **Dimensions**: Exact measurements (height, width, depth, diameter)
2. **Age**: Known or estimated period of creation
3. **Provenance**: History of ownership, including any documentation
4. **Condition**: Honest assessment of damage, repairs, or restoration
5. **Markings**: Any signatures, stamps, or labels (transcribe if legible)
6. **Material**: What the item is made of (wood type, metal, glass, etc.)
7. **Origin**: Where the item was made, if known

### Setting Realistic Expectations

Remember that online appraisals provide estimates based on limited information. Factors that might affect the accuracy include:

- Inability to physically examine the item
- Limited ability to authenticate through photos alone
- Fluctuating market conditions
- Regional value variations

Consider an online appraisal as a starting point rather than a definitive valuation, especially for potentially valuable or complex items.

### Research Before Submitting

Doing preliminary research can significantly enhance the quality of your appraisal:

1. **Identify Basic Category**: Determine if your item is furniture, jewelry, artwork, etc.

4. **Review Reference Books**: Consult price guides or collector reference books for your item category.

5. **Join Collector Groups**: Facebook groups and forums often provide insight into specific collectibles.

{{< content-modules/section-header title="Common Challenges with Free Online Appraisals" level="2" >}}
While free online appraisals offer tremendous convenience, users frequently encounter several challenges. Understanding these potential issues can help you navigate the process more effectively.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

### Delayed or No Responses

One of the most common frustrations with free appraisal services is waiting for responses that may arrive late or not at all. This typically occurs because:

- Free services often prioritize paying clients
- High submission volumes lead to backlogs
- Your item may fall outside their area of expertise
- Spam filters may block legitimate communications

**Solution:** Submit to multiple services simultaneously and follow up politely after 1-2 weeks if you haven't received a response.

### Limited Detail in Responses

Free appraisals sometimes provide minimal information or very broad value ranges. This can happen because:

- Without physical examination, experts must be cautious
- Detailed appraisals require time that isn't compensated
- Services may reserve comprehensive analysis for paying clients

**Solution:** Ask specific follow-up questions about factors that might increase value or areas that need clarification.

### Authentication Limitations

Online appraisals struggle with definitively authenticating items since physical inspection is often necessary to:

- Examine materials and construction methods
- Detect restoration or repairs
- Verify signatures or marks
- Assess condition thoroughly

**Solution:** Consider authentication services for high-value items where provenance significantly impacts worth.

### Potential Conflicts of Interest

Free appraisal services offered by auction houses or dealers may have inherent conflicts:

- Values might be influenced by the business's interest in acquiring items
- Estimates may emphasize retail replacement value over fair market value
- Services might downplay value to secure consignments at favorable terms

**Solution:** Seek multiple opinions, especially from sources without direct interest in acquiring your item.
