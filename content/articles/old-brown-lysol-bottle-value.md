{{< content-modules/section-header title="Identifying Vintage Brown Lysol Bottles" level="2" >}}
Learning to properly identify genuine vintage Lysol bottles is crucial for both collectors and sellers. Various characteristics can help determine the age and authenticity of these amber glass bottles.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

![Close-up of a vintage Lysol bottle showing embossed script lettering and manufacturer details](https://ik.imagekit.io/appraisily/SEO/old-brown-lysol-bottle-value/old-brown-lysol-bottle-value-image-2_VExpIAO-C.png)

### Key Identification Features

Most authentic vintage brown Lysol bottles share several distinguishing characteristics:

1. **Embossed Lettering**: Genuine bottles typically feature raised "Lysol" lettering in script font along the shoulder of the bottle.

2. **Manufacturer Marks**: Look for "L & F Prod Corp" (Lehn & Fink Products Corporation) and "Bloomfield, NJ" embossed on the bottle's base or side.

3. **Closure Types**: 
   - Cork tops typically indicate bottles from the 1910s-1930s
   - Screw tops generally date from the 1930s-1950s

4. **Glass Color**: Authentic bottles have a deep amber/brown color, intended to protect the contents from light degradation.

5. **Size Variations**: Common sizes include 2oz, 4oz, and 8oz versions, with larger bottles typically being more valuable.

{{< content-modules/section-header title="Collecting Vintage Lysol Bottles" level="2" >}}
For those interested in starting or expanding a collection of vintage brown Lysol bottles, there are several approaches to consider.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

### Collection Themes

1. **Chronological Collections**: Gathering examples from each decade to show the evolution of the design

2. **Size Variations**: Collecting different sizes from the smallest 2oz bottles to larger household sizes

3. **Regional Variations**: Some bottles have different embossing patterns or manufacturing locations

4. **Complete Sets**: Seeking bottles with original labels, packaging, and advertising materials

### Collection Care Tips

1. **Cleaning**: Use only warm water and mild soap to clean vintage glass bottles. Avoid harsh chemicals that could damage embossing or residual labels.

2. **Display**: Keep bottles out of direct sunlight to prevent the amber glass from fading or developing a purplish tint.

3. **Storage**: Store bottles upright in a stable environment with consistent temperature and humidity.

4. **Documentation**: Keep records of purchase dates, prices, and any known provenance for each bottle in your collection.

Understanding the historical significance of Lysol and its packaging adds substantial depth to a collection of vintage brown bottles.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

### The Lysol Brand History

Lysol was first formulated in Germany in the late 19th century as a response to a cholera epidemic. The name derives from "lysol" (from the Greek "lysis" meaning to dissolve) and "ol" (from the alcohol content). It was introduced to the American market in the early 1900s by Lehn & Fink Products Corporation.

Initially marketed primarily as a household disinfectant, Lysol was promoted for a wide range of uses, from cleaning floors to questionable personal hygiene applications. The amber glass bottles protected the light-sensitive formula from degradation.

### Cultural Significance

The distinctive brown Lysol bottles became a common sight in American homes throughout the early and mid-20th century. They represented the growing awareness of germ theory and the increasing emphasis on household cleanliness and disinfection that characterized the era.

The bottles themselves showcase the evolution of pharmaceutical packaging and early branded consumer products, making them important artifacts of American consumer culture.

    Proper cleaning of vintage Lysol bottles requires gentle techniques to preserve their value:
    
    1. **Never use harsh chemicals or abrasives** that could damage the glass or embossing
    2. **Start with warm water and mild soap**, using a soft bottle brush for the interior

Whether you're a dedicated bottle collector, pharmaceutical memorabilia enthusiast, or simply appreciate vintage household items, these bottles represent an accessible collecting category with moderate value and rich historical significance.
