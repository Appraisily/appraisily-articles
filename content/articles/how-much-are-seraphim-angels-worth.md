{{< content-modules/section-header title="How to Identify Your Seraphim Angels" level="2" >}}
Proper identification is crucial for determining the value of your Seraphim Angels. Each figurine has distinctive characteristics that can help you identify its model, age, and potential worth.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

### Finding Model Information

Look for these identifying features on your Seraphim Angel figurines:

1. **Base Markings**: Check the bottom of the figurine for inscriptions including:
   - The name of the angel
   - "Roman, Inc." or "Seraphim Classics" markings
   - Production year
   - Limited edition numbering (if applicable)

2. **Certificate of Authenticity**: Original certificates include:
   - The angel's name and description
   - Production details
   - Limited edition information
   - Occasionally, the suggested retail price

3. **Original Box**: The packaging typically features:
   - Clear images of the specific angel
   - Complete name and model number
   - Special edition designations

4. **Tags**: Hang tags often include:
   - The angel's name and story
   - Unique product code
   - Release date information

By carefully examining these elements, you can precisely identify your Seraphim Angel model, which is the first step in determining its current market value.

{{< content-modules/section-header title="Authenticating Seraphim Angels" level="2" >}}
Given their popularity, Seraphim Angels can sometimes be confused with similar figurines or imitations. Here's how to verify that you have an authentic Seraphim Classics piece.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

### Key Authentication Factors

1. **Quality of Materials**: Authentic Seraphim Angels are made from high-quality porcelain with a natural finish. The material should have a slight translucency when held up to light.

2. **Craftsmanship**: The details should be crisp and well-defined, particularly in facial features, hair, wings, and clothing folds. Poor or simplistic detailing may indicate a reproduction.

3. **Base Markings**: All authentic pieces bear the "Roman, Inc." or "Seraphim Classics" mark, along with the figurine's name and often a production date or number.

4. **Weight**: Genuine Seraphim Angels have a substantial feel due to their porcelain and marble composition.

5. **Packaging**: Original boxes feature high-quality printing with clear images of the specific angel and detailed information.

By examining these elements carefully, you can distinguish authentic Seraphim Classics from similar figurines produced by other manufacturers or potential counterfeits.

3. **Condition Preservation**: To maintain investment potential, figurines must be kept in pristine condition, preferably with their original packaging and documentation intact.

4. **Market Trends**: The collectibles market is cyclical. Items that are less fashionable today may see renewed interest in future decades.

5. **Passion Premium**: Collecting items you genuinely appreciate ensures you'll value your collection regardless of market fluctuations.

For serious collectors, focusing on limited editions, special event pieces, and discontinued models offers the best potential for long-term value appreciation. However, most experts recommend collecting Seraphim Angels primarily for enjoyment rather than as financial investments.

{{< content-modules/section-header title="Caring for Your Seraphim Angels Collection" level="2" >}}
Proper care and storage are essential for maintaining both the beauty and value of your Seraphim Angels. These delicate figurines require specific handling and display techniques to preserve their condition.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

### Cleaning and Maintenance

1. **Dusting**: Use a soft, clean makeup brush or photographer's brush to gently remove dust from delicate areas. Avoid feather dusters which can catch on small details.

2. **Cleaning**: If necessary, use a slightly damp, soft cloth for surface cleaning. Never submerge in water or use chemical cleaners.

3. **Handling**: Always hold Seraphim Angels by their base, not by delicate parts like wings or extended arms.

4. **Repairs**: If damage occurs, seek professional restoration rather than attempting DIY repairs which can significantly reduce value.

### Display and Storage

1. **Light Exposure**: Keep figurines away from direct sunlight, which can fade colors over time.

2. **Temperature and Humidity**: Maintain consistent conditions; avoid areas with high humidity or temperature fluctuations that can stress the porcelain.

3. **Secure Shelving**: Use display cabinets with glass doors to prevent dust accumulation while displaying your collection.

4. **Storage Boxes**: When not on display, store in original boxes with acid-free tissue paper for padding.

5. **Documentation Storage**: Keep certificates of authenticity and other paperwork in archival-quality sleeves or folders, separate from the figurines but easily accessible.

By following these care guidelines, you'll help ensure your Seraphim Angels remain in excellent condition, preserving both their beauty and market value for years to come.
