Louis Icart (1888-1950) stands as one of the most celebrated Art Deco artists of the early 20th century. His elegant, often erotic depictions of fashionable women and pastoral scenes have captivated collectors for generations. If you've discovered what might be an Icart piece or are considering investing in his work, understanding the current market values and authentication process is essential.

This comprehensive guide will explore the factors affecting Louis Icart paintings' value, how to identify authentic works, recent auction results, and investment considerations for collectors.

{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

Louis Justin Laurent Icart was born in Toulouse, France in 1888. He began drawing at an early age and moved to Paris in 1907, where he studied at the Acad√©mie Julian. Icart's artistic career flourished during the 1920s and 1930s, particularly in New York City where his Art Deco style resonated with American audiences.

Icart is primarily known for his etchings and engravings rather than oil paintings, though he worked in both mediums. His signature style features elegant, fashionable women in romantic or mildly erotic scenarios, often with flowing dresses, and frequently accompanied by animals such as dogs (particularly greyhounds) or birds.

### Signature Verification

Icart's signature evolved over time, which can help date a piece. His early works (pre-1920) typically show "Louis Icart" in a flowing script. Later works often feature a more stylized "Icart" with a distinctive capital "I" and flourish on the final "t".

### Plate Marks and Blind Stamps

One of the most reliable authentication methods for Icart etchings is the presence of a plate mark. Since Icart used thin plates, both the top and bottom edges of the plate can be visible in the paper. This mark is created during the printing process and must be present on genuine etchings.

Many authentic Icart prints also feature a blind stamp or embossing, often from publishers like F. Champagne or Les Graveurs Modernes.

### Paper Quality and Aging

Authentic Icart prints were typically produced on high-quality papers that age in specific ways. Modern reproductions often use contemporary papers that won't demonstrate the same aging characteristics as original works from the 1920s and 1930s.

As [Lockport Street Gallery](https://lockportstreetgallery.com/icart/louis-icart-authenticity/icartinfo2/) notes: "The plate mark is created during the printing process, and has to be there if the work is an etching as opposed to a reproduction lithographic print."

{{< content-modules/section-header title="Factors Affecting Louis Icart Artwork Value" level="2" >}}
Several key factors influence the market value of Louis Icart's paintings, etchings, and prints.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

### Subject Matter

Icart's most valuable works typically feature:
- Elegant women in Art Deco fashion
- Mildly erotic or romantic scenes
- Greyhound dogs (a signature motif)
- Parisian scenes
- Art Deco styling and composition

Works featuring these elements typically command premium prices compared to his more conventional landscapes or pastoral scenes.

![Louis Icart artwork featuring his signature style elements](https://ik.imagekit.io/appraisily/SEO/louis-icart-paintings-value/louis-icart-paintings-value-image-5-error_fYHmiXN75.png)

### Medium and Technique

Icart worked in several mediums, each with its own value hierarchy:

1. **Original Oil Paintings**: The rarest and most valuable Icart works
2. **Original Color Etchings**: His most recognized and collected works
3. **Limited Edition Prints**: Authorized prints from original plates
4. **Drawings and Studies**: Original preparatory works on paper
5. **Modern Reproductions**: Little investment value

### Condition Considerations

Condition dramatically impacts value. Common condition issues include:

- Paper discoloration or foxing
- Fading of colors (especially reds)
- Tears or repairs
- Trimmed margins
- Frame damage
- Improper restoration attempts

Pieces in excellent original condition with vibrant colors can command prices 40-60% higher than those with significant condition issues.

### Provenance and Documentation

Artwork with documented history or provenance from reputable galleries or collections typically achieves higher prices. Documentation might include:

- Gallery certificates of authenticity
- Previous auction records
- Exhibition history
- Publications featuring the work
- Original purchase receipts
