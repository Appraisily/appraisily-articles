## Introduction to the 2020 Bat Quarter

The 2020 "Bat Quarter" has gained significant attention from both casual coin finders and serious collectors. Released as part of the America the Beautiful Quarters Program, this coin represents the National Park of American Samoa and features a striking design of a Samoan fruit bat mother hanging with her pup. Released just before the COVID-19 pandemic, the timing of a coin featuring a bat created additional interest and even some superstition around this particular quarter.

But what is this coin actually worth? Whether you've found one in your pocket change or you're considering purchasing one for your collection, understanding the true value of the 2020 Bat Quarter requires knowledge of mint marks, errors, and condition grades.

{{< content-modules/section-header title="The Basics of the 2020 Bat Quarter" level="2" >}}
Before diving into specific values, it's important to understand what makes this quarter unique and the different varieties available.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

The 2020 American Samoa Quarter (commonly called the "Bat Quarter") was the 51st coin released in the America the Beautiful Quarters Program, which ran from 2010 to 2021. The reverse design depicts a Samoan fruit bat mother hanging in a tree with her pup, representing the National Park of American Samoa's unique ecological features.

![2020 Bat Quarter reverse design showing details of the bat mother and pup](https://ik.imagekit.io/appraisily/SEO/how-much-is-the-2020-bat-quarter-worth/how-much-is-the-2020-bat-quarter-worth-image-2_OjcP5ziPc.png)

These quarters were minted at four different U.S. Mint facilities, each with its own mint mark:
- P (Philadelphia)
- D (Denver)
- S (San Francisco) - proof versions
- W (West Point) - limited special issues

Most 2020 Bat Quarters found in circulation are from the P or D mints. The S mint produced proof coins specifically for collectors, while the rare W mint coins were specially released into circulation as part of a promotion to encourage coin collecting.

- **P Mint:** 234,800,000 coins produced
- **D Mint:** 272,400,000 coins produced
- **W Mint:** Only 2,000,000 coins produced

### Other Notable Errors

Beyond the "Blind Bat" error, collectors should look for:

1. **Clad Layer Missing** - Where some or all of the outer copper-nickel layer is missing, exposing the inner copper core
2. **Double Die Errors** - Where parts of the design appear doubled due to multiple strikes
3. **Off-Center Strikes** - Where the design is not centered on the coin blank
4. **Die Cracks and Cuds** - Raised lines or bumps resulting from damaged dies

{{< content-modules/section-header title="Factors That Determine Value" level="2" >}}
Multiple factors affect the value of a 2020 Bat Quarter beyond just its mint mark or errors.
{{< /content-modules/section-header >}}

When assessing the value of your 2020 Bat Quarter, consider these important factors:

### 1. Mint Mark

As we've discussed, the mint mark (P, D, S, or W) significantly impacts value, with W being the most valuable.

### 2. Condition Grade

Coin grading follows a standard scale:
- **Circulated**: Grades from About Good (AG-3) to Extremely Fine (EF-45)
- **Uncirculated**: Mint State grades from MS-60 to MS-70 (perfect)
- **Proof**: Proof grades from PR-60 to PR-70 (perfect)

Higher grades result in higher values, especially for uncirculated and proof examples.

### 3. Error Type and Severity

The type of error and how dramatic it appears can greatly impact value. Minor die cracks might add a small premium, while major errors like the "Blind Bat" or wrong planchet strikes can add hundreds of dollars in value.

### 4. Certification

Coins certified and graded by professional services like PCGS (Professional Coin Grading Service) or NGC (Numismatic Guaranty Corporation) typically command higher prices than raw (ungraded) examples, especially for higher grades and error varieties.

## Conclusion: Is Your 2020 Bat Quarter Worth Keeping?

The 2020 Bat Quarter presents an interesting case study in modern coin collecting. While most examples found in circulation are worth only face value or a slight premium, certain varieties—particularly those with the W mint mark or notable errors—can be worth significantly more.

If you've discovered a 2020 Bat Quarter in your change:

1. **Check the mint mark** first - a W mint mark immediately makes it worth keeping
2. **Examine for errors** - particularly the "Blind Bat" error where the face is missing
3. **Assess the condition** - even common mint marks in pristine uncirculated condition can be worth preserving

For most people, finding a W mint mark Bat Quarter in circulation would be the equivalent of finding a $10-20 bill on the sidewalk—a nice bonus, but not life-changing. However, major error varieties can be worth hundreds of dollars, making this modern quarter worth watching for in your pocket change.

Whether you're a serious collector or just curious about a coin you've found, the 2020 Bat Quarter represents an accessible entry point into the fascinating world of modern coin collecting, where even today's coins can hold surprising value.
