Introduction to Chris DeRubeis Art Value

Chris DeRubeis has revolutionized contemporary art with his groundbreaking Abstract Sensualism® style, creating dynamic metal-based works that command significant attention in the art market. His innovative fusion of sculpture and painting on metal, combined with proprietary techniques involving chemicals, power tools, and natural elements like fire and ice, has established him as a unique voice in the art world.

The value of DeRubeis' artwork stems from several key factors: his technical innovation, emotional resonance, and cultural impact. Each piece represents an irreplicable artistic achievement due to the unpredictable chemical reactions and hand-ground metal surfaces that define his style. This combination of technical mastery and emotional depth positions his work as both collectible and investment-grade.

Historical Development and Career Trajectory

Chris DeRubeis' Artistic Journey

1978

Early Beginnings

Born in 1978 in Southern California, Chris DeRubeis showed artistic talent from an early age.

Early 1990s

First Artistic Ventures

By age 13, DeRubeis was using his grandfather's airbrush to paint motorcycles, demonstrating early technical skill.

Late 1990s

Formal Education

Attended Pasadena Art Center and Associates in Art school, funding his studies through custom Harley-Davidson paintings.

Early 2000s

Abstract Sensualism® Development

Developed his signature style after noticing light patterns on a grinded motorcycle gas tank, leading to experimentation with metal as an artistic medium.

2005

Major Recognition

Won the prestigious MUSE Award for "New Movement" at the First Annual Fine Art Awards, establishing his place in contemporary art.

This evolutionary path from motorcycle painter to internationally recognized artist has significantly impacted the value of DeRubeis' work. His 2005 MUSE Award particularly stands as a milestone that elevated his market position and cemented his reputation as an innovator.

Types and Categories of DeRubeis Artwork

DeRubeis' artwork can be categorized in several ways, each affecting its market value:

By Medium

DeRubeis primarily works with metal, but the specific type of metal significantly impacts the artwork's value:

  • Aircraft aluminum (most common)
  • Silver
  • Gold (highest value)
  • Brass
  • Copper

By Size and Format

The scale of his works ranges considerably:

  • Small pieces (e.g., Mini Burst series at approximately 20×20 inches)
  • Medium works (single panels 24×36 inches)
  • Large installations (multi-panel works spanning several feet)
  • Quadtych arrangements (four coordinated panels)

By Technique

His distinctive techniques create varying visual effects:

  • Chemical reactions with metal
  • Fire-treated surfaces
  • Water and ice textures
  • Grinding patterns
  • Multi-layered urethane coatings

Frequently Asked Questions About Chris DeRubeis Art

How much is Chris DeRubeis art worth?

Chris DeRubeis artwork varies significantly in value based on several factors. Recent auction records show smaller works selling between $1,000-$1,700, while medium-sized pieces typically range from $1,800-$4,500. His signature abstract works on metal command $3,000-$8,000, with large multi-panel installations reaching $15,000 or more. Pieces incorporating precious metals like gold or silver can exceed $20,000. The artwork's size, material, technique, provenance, and condition all significantly impact its market value.

How can I tell if a Chris DeRubeis artwork is authentic?

Authenticating a Chris DeRubeis artwork involves several verification points:

  1. Look for his distinctive metal treatment techniques including unique grinding patterns and chemical reactions
  2. Check for a certificate of authenticity from a reputable gallery like Park West
  3. Examine for his signature, typically on the back or edge of the metal
  4. Verify the artwork has the characteristic protective urethane coating
  5. Confirm the materials match the description (aircraft aluminum, gold, silver, etc.)

For valuable pieces, consulting a professional art appraiser specializing in contemporary metal art is recommended.

Where can I buy or sell Chris DeRubeis art?

Chris DeRubeis artwork can be purchased or sold through several reputable channels:

For buying new works:

  • DeRubeis Fine Art Gallery in Key West
  • Park West Gallery (both physical locations and cruise ship galleries)
  • Wyecliffe Galleries (UK)

For secondary market purchases or sales:

  • Art Brokerage
  • Auction houses like Invaluable, MutualArt, and specialized fine art auctioneers
  • 1stDibs and other curated online marketplaces

Always verify authenticity and provenance documentation before purchasing.
